* Learn the process of purchasing a domain, integrating it with Route 53, and managing centralized DNS on AWS.

### Week 3 Achievements:

* Understood the process of purchasing a domain and integrating it with Route 53 for centralized DNS management on AWS.
* Grasped the concepts, roles, and types of DNS records in Route 53 (A, CNAME, NS, MX, ...).
* Learned about the hybrid DNS model, combining on-premises DNS and Route 53, and practical application scenarios.
* Learned how to launch and configure a virtual server to deploy internal DNS services.
* Understood the operating principles of Load Balancer, the types of AWS Load Balancers (ALB, NLB, CLB), and how Route 53 distributes traffic to endpoints.
* Understood the role of Route 53 in connecting, load balancing, and ensuring high availability for the system.
* Learned how to use Route 53 health checks and DNS failover to automatically redirect traffic when an endpoint fails.
* Understood common integration patterns between Route 53, Load Balancer, and backend services (EC2, ECS, Lambda, S3 static website, ...).
* Grasped best practices for multi-AZ and multi-region partitioning to increase availability and scalability for DNS and applications.
* Identified key factors for building real-world project ideas on AWS, especially HA/DR, security, and cost optimization when designing systems with Route 53 and Load Balancer.
